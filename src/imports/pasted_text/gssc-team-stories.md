## **USC — Atlix**

**GSSC Worlds 2026 — Seoul, May 17–21**

Every major political crisis, market disruption, and cultural flashpoint of the last decade started the same way — as a signal in online discourse that nobody was watching. The January 6 insurrection didn't emerge overnight. The narratives that fueled it were clustering across Facebook groups, Twitter threads, and YouTube channels for months. By the time institutions could see it, it was too late to respond.

A team from USC is building the infrastructure to close that gap. Atlix is a Bloomberg Terminal for cultural narratives — a real-time intelligence platform that ingests public data across news, social media, and online discourse, then uses AI to detect emerging topics, measure sentiment and emotion, identify polarization between communities, and track how narratives spread across platforms and borders. Not for marketers. For policymakers, NGOs, and institutions that need to understand what's happening before it becomes a crisis. The team is working with a mentor from Edison International.

## **Harvard — Acre**

**GSSC Worlds 2026 — Seoul, May 17–21**

India. Nigeria. Southeast Asia. Millions of smallholder farmers lose entire harvests to drought and flooding every year. When that happens, they don't just lose income — they lose the capital to plant the next season. One bad climate event can trap a family for years.

A team from Harvard is building a way out. Acre is a satellite-triggered parametric insurance platform — when a drought or flood crosses a defined threshold, verified by satellite data, farmers receive an automatic payout. No claims process. No waiting. No paperwork. The money arrives when it's needed most. The team is working with a mentor from Asesoria Partners.

## **University of Oxford — Health Atlas**

## **Team Europe — Digital Alibi**

**GSSC Worlds 2026 — Seoul, May 17–21**

Every day, people are being placed in locations they've never been. In videos they never appeared in. In photos that were never taken. Deepfakes and AI-generated content have made fabricated evidence indistinguishable from the real thing — and most victims have no way to fight back. The harm is immediate. The ability to respond is almost nonexistent.

Team Europe is building the defense. Digital Alibi transforms the passive sensor data already living on your phone into cryptographically verified, tamper-proof proof of reality. GPS logs, Wi-Fi connections, motion data, photo metadata — cross-referenced and sealed with a cryptographic hash that you own and control. When a deepfake surfaces showing you somewhere you weren't, you have irrefutable evidence that says otherwise. When a fabricated photo appears, the cryptographic record simply doesn't exist — because the moment never happened. The team is working with a mentor from OpenAI.

## **POSTECH — !nsight (TASTE)**

**GSSC Worlds 2026 — Korean Team Prize: Silicon Valley**

Gen Z creates more personal data than any generation in history — photos, GPS logs, notes, browsing patterns, location check-ins. Almost none of it is ever looked at again. It sits in galleries and cloud drives, archived but never understood. The result is what the team calls the Vibe Gap — people have the records of their lives but no way to synthesize what those records actually say about them.

A team from POSTECH is building the tool to close it. TASTE uses AI to transform forgotten photo logs and fragmented personal data into personalized lifestyle insights — a Vibe Report that feels less like a data summary and more like a private digital magazine. By cross-referencing photos, notes, and GPS data together rather than analyzing them in isolation, the platform finds patterns in a user's real taste that they might not recognize themselves. The team is working with a mentor from Ticketmaster.

## **Ewha — Now or Never**

**GSSC Worlds 2026 — Korean Team Prize: Silicon Valley**

Outdoor livestreaming is broken. A two-hour stream consumes around 8GB of mobile data — costing creators roughly $70 per stream. Most respond by going indoors or switching to pre-recorded content. The spontaneous, real-time connection between creators and their audiences disappears, replaced by edited, curated content that arrives hours or days later.

A team from Ewha is rebuilding that connection. Their platform lets fans send data credits directly to a creator's livestream in real time — visible as a live data bar on screen, replenished by the community that wants the stream to continue. Paired with an affordable eSIM-based bonding device that dramatically lowers the cost of professional outdoor streaming hardware, the platform redistributes both the cost and the infrastructure of live content creation across the people who care most about it. The team is working with a mentor from TikTok Live.

## **Ewha — aim**

**GSSC Worlds 2026 — Korean Team Prize: Silicon Valley**

Hiring is still broken at the earliest stage. Companies make critical decisions about early-career candidates based on resumes, portfolios, and short interviews — formats that do a poor job of predicting how someone actually works. The result is expensive mismatches, high first-year attrition, and a persistent gap between what candidates present and what employers actually need to know.

A team from Ewha is replacing the resume with something better. aim is a recruitment simulation platform that places candidates directly into a realistic version of the role — a structured task built around how the company's best performers actually work, evaluated against a competency framework of 15 core skills and 265 sub-indicators. Employers get behavioral evidence. Candidates get a genuine preview of what they're walking into. Both sides make better decisions. The team is working with a mentor from Techstars.

## **Dongseo — Touch of Love**

**GSSC Worlds 2026 — Korean Team Prize: Silicon Valley**

Thirty-four percent of Korean households are single-person. For many of them, a pet isn't a pet — it's family. But one of the most fundamental human experiences, sharing a meal, has always been off limits. What you eat and what your pet eats have never been the same thing, and that barrier creates a quiet but real emotional distance at the moment that's supposed to bring people together.

A team from Dongseo is breaking that barrier. Touch of Love — Hands of Love — has created the world's first shared pairing dessert system using 100% Yeongdo sweet potatoes, a local heritage crop from the Busan region. The desserts are formulated to be safe, high quality, and genuinely delicious for both humans and their pets simultaneously. One bite for you. One bite for them. The emotional experience of sharing food, made possible for the first time. The team is working with a mentor from Frontrow Global.
