WHAT IS GSSC WORLDS
Section Label: About the Competition
Headline: Where Regional Champions Become Global Founders
Body:
GSSC Worlds is the culmination of GSSF's annual cycle — a fully funded, in-person global competition that brings together the top undergraduate founding teams from universities across six continents.
Selected through a rigorous regional qualification process, every team that reaches the Worlds stage has already proven themselves at home. Seoul is where the best of the best compete for global recognition, mentorship from world-class leaders, and a place in the GSSF ecosystem.
This is not a virtual pitch competition. Every element — the Pitch Bootcamp, the Preliminary Round, and the Finals — happens entirely in person.

HOW IT WORKS
Section Label: The Format
Headline: From Campus to Seoul in Four Stages
Stage 1 — Compete Regionally
Teams compete at their home university or regional GSSC competition. Regional champions earn a fully funded trip to GSSC Worlds — travel, lodging, and all programming covered by GSSF.
Stage 2 — Pitch Bootcamp (Seoul)
Arriving in Seoul, teams enter a two-day intensive Pitch Bootcamp. Working directly with GSSF mentors, teams refine their narrative, sharpen their pitch, and prepare for the global stage.
Stage 3 — Preliminary Round
All teams compete in the Preliminary Round, where they are evaluated against the GSSC Competition Standard — GSSF's proprietary merit benchmark ensuring world-class quality across every team on the global stage.
Stage 4 — Finals at the Asian Leadership Conference
Top teams advance to the Finals, held on stage at the Asian Leadership Conference (ALC) — one of Asia's most prominent leadership and innovation summits — in front of a global audience of leaders, investors, and changemakers.

WHO QUALIFIES
Section Label: Eligibility
Headline: Earn Your Spot
Body:
GSSC Worlds is not an open application. Teams qualify by winning their regional GSSC competition or through the GSSF Academy and Global AI Cup pathways. Every team on the Worlds stage has been vetted against the same global standard.
Eligibility at a Glance:

Teams of 2–5 current undergraduate students
English-language participation required
No incorporated companies, no accelerator funding, no significant revenue
Venture work must have begun no earlier than June 1 of the qualifying year
Full in-person attendance required for all programming in Seoul


THE GSSF COMMITMENT
Section Label: Fully Funded
Headline: Geographic Location Should Not Limit Access to the Global Stage
Body:
Every qualifying team receives full funding to compete at GSSC Worlds. GSSF covers international travel, lodging, and all programming costs — because the best founders in the world should not be disqualified by their zip code.
This is the Full-Scholarship Model in practice.
